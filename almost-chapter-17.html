Chapter 17

The Almost Relapse

It happened on an ordinary Tuesday. No warning. No obvious trigger. Just a small moment that could have gone unnoticed— if they had still been who they used to be. Elena had sent him a message around noon.

Elena: My tutorial ended early. Want to grab lunch?

An hour passed. No reply. Two hours. Still nothing. She told herself he was busy. Engineering labs. Dead phone. Group work. But her chest tightened anyway. Old memories don’t disappear just because you’ve grown. They wait for familiar patterns. By the third hour, her mind had already begun its quiet spiral. This is how it starts. He’s pulling back. You misread everything. She hated how quickly the thoughts arrived. Growth, she realized, doesn’t erase fear. It just gives you a choice about what to do with it.

At 3:47 p.m., her phone buzzed.

Adrian: I’m so sorry. My phone died after lab and I just got back to my room. Lunch still possible?

She stared at the message. Relief washed over her first. Then something else. Annoyance. Not at him. At how fast her trust had trembled. She typed back carefully.

Elena: It’s okay. I figured you were busy.

A pause. Then another message from him.

Adrian: Are you sure?

She hesitated. This was the moment. The old version of her would have said yes. Pretended nothing happened. Swallowed the discomfort to keep things smooth. Instead, she typed:

Elena: It just brought up some old feelings. That’s all.

She stared at the screen after sending it. Her heart pounded louder than it should have for something so small.

Five minutes later, her phone rang. She blinked. He was calling. She answered.

“Hey,” she said.

“Hey,” he replied. His voice sounded slightly breathless. “I don’t want you sitting with that alone.”

The words surprised her. “I wasn’t spiraling,” she said lightly.

“Maybe not fully,” he said. “But I know I’ve trained you to expect silence.”

The honesty caught her off guard. She sat down on the edge of her bed. “I don’t want to react to you like you’re still the old version of yourself,” she admitted. “But sometimes my body doesn’t know the difference yet.”

“That makes sense,” he said softly. “I’d probably feel the same if the roles were reversed.” There was no defensiveness in his tone. Just understanding.

This is the fragile part. Not the big conversations. Not the confessions. The tiny moments where history tries to rewrite the present. I don’t want to punish him for who he used to be. But I also don’t want to ignore the version of me who remembers. Trust isn’t a switch. It’s a muscle. And muscles shake before they strengthen.

“I’m not pulling away,” he said clearly. “And if I ever need space, I’ll say it. Not disappear.”

She let that settle. “Okay,” she said.

“Okay,” he echoed.

A quiet silence followed. Comfortable. Grounded. “Lunch tomorrow?” he asked gently.

She smiled despite herself. “Tomorrow works.”

That evening, Elena lay in bed thinking about the day. Nothing dramatic had happened. No arguments. No declarations. Just a moment that could have reopened an old wound. But it hadn’t. Because this time, they didn’t let silence fill the gap. They spoke. And in speaking, something shifted.

Maybe this is what healing inside a relationship looks like. Not perfection. Not flawless trust. But catching the almost. The almost assumption. The almost retreat. The almost collapse into old patterns. And choosing differently. Today wasn’t grand. It was small. But small things, I’m learning, are where love either quietly breaks or quietly rebuilds itself. And today— we rebuilt.
